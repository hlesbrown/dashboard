April Theme Sacred Rain April is the month when the sky opens. The rains that fall now aren't winter's cold assault but something gentler and more deliberate — water that softens the ground, calls seeds upward, and washes winter's residue from everything it touches. This month we ask: what in us is waiting to be softened? What would grow if we stopped protecting it from the rain?

From the Editor Letting the Rain In By Les Brown There's a kind of courage in standing unprotected in a downpour — not running for shelter, not shielding yourself, but simply allowing the water to find every surface of your skin. That's what this month's theme asks of us. Sacred rain isn't gentle. It doesn't ask permission. It arrives, and in its arrival, it reveals what we've been holding too tightly.

We spend so much of our lives building shelter — emotional shelter, physical shelter, the armor of competence and control. And those shelters serve us. They keep us upright through storms we didn't choose. But there comes a time when the shelter itself becomes the problem. When the walls we built to protect our tenderness begin to imprison it instead…

When rain meets skin, there is no negotiation — only contact.
